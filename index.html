Student journalism

My name is Francis Szetela. I'm currently enrolled at the Colorado State University journalism program online and expecting to graduate in the summer of 2024.

I live in South Seattle and I am interested in reporting on local climate resilience and adaptation strategies, as well as reporting on the latest science and technology advances through an ethical framework.

I am driven to continue my education by keeping abreast of changes to the media landscape, and adopting useful tools and knowledge for fighting mis- and disinformation.

I believe in total transparency in journalism, and aim to make clear in my writing how and why I investigate the topics and stories I pursue. I believe in approaching journalism with the rigor of a scientist, and producing knowledge that reflects the full breadth and complexity of peoples’ lives and experiences.

Below you’ll find some of my recent work. More can be found in the portfolio section of this website. Please read more about me here, including my personal code of ethics.

Commitment to Social & Environmental Justice

I am committed to maintaining the latest in language use, out of respect for the identities and experiences of all people, and with special consideration for systemic injustices which result in the marginalization, under representation, and erasure of voices and communities.

I believe in the power of language to shape perceptions and seek to identify and eliminate biased language in my own writing.

I am committed to interrogating and dismantling systems of historical oppression, including colonialism and white supremacy, both within myself and in our communities and institutions.
